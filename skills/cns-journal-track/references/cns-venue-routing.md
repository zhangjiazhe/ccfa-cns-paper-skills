# CNS Venue Routing

Use this when deciding among Nature, Science, Cell, and their subjournals.

## Main-Journal Fit

Prefer main Nature/Science/Cell only when:
- the contribution is conceptually broad, not just technically strong
- the story matters outside a narrow specialty
- the central result can be explained to non-specialist scientific readers
- the evidence package supports a full, high-confidence narrative

## Nature Family

Strong fit when the story is broad science, method-enabling, biomedical, computational biology, or resource-centered.

Examples:
- `Nature`: broad conceptual advance
- `Nature Methods`: enabling method, assay, computational platform, validation and reproducibility
- `Nature Biotechnology`: biotechnology or translational significance, platform utility, engineering depth
- `Nature Machine Intelligence`: AI/ML advance with broader machine-intelligence significance

## Science Family

Strong fit when the story is compact, broadly important, and editorially legible to a general science audience. Science-family subjournals may fit more specialized translational, robotics, or advances-style stories.

## Cell Family

Strong fit when the paper is biology/medicine/mechanism/resource rich and the figure story is central. Cell-family subjournals can fit systems, genomics, translational, neuroscience, molecular, and computational biology stories.

## Article-Type Decision

Resolve early:
- Article: linked claims with substantial evidence chain
- Resource: reusable dataset/platform/atlas/screen with community value
- Method: enabling protocol/tool with validation and reproducibility
- Analysis: integrative analytical insight rather than new experimental method

If the paper oscillates between method and resource, decide before rewriting abstract or Results.
